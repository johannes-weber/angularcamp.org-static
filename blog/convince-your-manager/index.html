Convince your manager

Posted: May 16, 2016

Convince your manager in order to attend AngularCamp

You want to attend AngularCamp happening in Barcelona from July 1-3, 2016, but don’t know how to bring it up to your manager (the person with the money)? We laid out a five major reasons and a sample letter explaining why it is important. Use it in full or use snippets of it, whatever it takes to get you to AngularCamp Barcelona!


> Five Reasons to Attend AngularCamp:
  1. Learn what works (and what doesn’t) in the real world. Let someone else’s hard-earned experience be your guide. With case studies and tales from the trenches, you’ll learn best practices, save time, and dodge the pitfalls that have vexed others.
  2. Be part of the conversation shaping the Web. AngularCamp is where important conversations take place and where big ideas explode into being. You’ll come away inspired and eager to test what you’ve learned.
  3. Make valuable connections. AngularCamp is designed to make networking easy, even if networking doesn’t come naturally to you. A large conference with a small-conference feel, AngularCamp provides ample opportunities to meet and compare experiences with others.
  4. Aim for the future. Be among the first to hear about emerging technologies and explore what’s on the horizon with the visionaries of the web platform.
  5. There will be a lot of sessions in the three days of AngularCamp. These sessions include visionary keynotes, hands-on tutorials, deep-dive technical lectures, and presentations.


> Letter

Dear [ManagersName],

I’d like to attend AngularCamp Conference taking place July 1-3, 2015, in Barcelona. There are a lot of Angular conferences during the year, but this is the premier conference put on for and by the the community around it. Vendor-neutral by design, it is focused on everything from fine-tuning my skills in AngularJS to the future of the framework.

They are anticipating more than 300 individuals will attend the event. The conference welcomes experienced developers as well as those interested in learning how to implement AngularJS into their business, and everyone in between. Here are just a few advantages of me going to the conference:

  1. One of the great features about the technology is how diverse it is. It can be used to build mobile apps or enterprise apps, and almost every single big named company is using it from PayPal to Google to Disney. If we decide to build out our product strategy or offering, it is likely that AngularJS will be the technology that I use across the board.

  2. There will be more than 30 sessions in the three day conference. These sessions include visionary keynotes, hands-on tutorials, deep-dive technical lectures, and presentations.

  3. The session will not only craft my skills with AngularJS, but also help me learn how best to adopt the technology to our business for smoother transitions and a more united Developer team.

  4. Key session include: Angular 2.0 with realtime GraphQL, Progressive Web Apps with Angular 2, From the World-Wide Web to the World of IoT, TypeScript fundamentals,… The full list of program can be found here: http://angularcamp.org/schedule/

The registration price is € 320. It includes registration and full access to all of the events happening at the AngularCamp.

I would be happy to write a post-conference report and share with other members of the team on what I’ve learned and how we can implement Angular as well as open source governing practices into our business strategy.

Thanks, [ YourName ]
